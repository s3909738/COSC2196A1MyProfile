Interests in IT:

I have interests in IT relating to computers, programming, game design, the Internet of Things, the Raspberry Pi and other SOC technologies. Being a creative minded person, I have always been fascinated with how IT applications can be used to customise and personalize interfaces and user experiences, as well as automate and simplify tasks. I have used graphics software to make icons and learned how to make my own Star Trek themed boot screen with the Grub2 boot loader.

I enjoy learning about the history of technological development and advances, and playing around with old, obsolete technology and programs. For instance, I explored using DOS 6.22 while reading "Special Edition Using MS-DOS® 6.22, Third Edition" by Jim Cooper, becoming familiar with batch files and command line programs.

Playing the LittleBigPlanet games on the PlayStation platform sparked and interest in programming and game design. With LittleBigPlanet 2 introducing me to logic gates. I would use the level creation to play around with logic and build all sorts of contraptions to use in levels. Some projects included: building a walking robot, building an interactive piano and I created a duck hunt game using motion controls. I enjoyed using the level creation tools to come up with solutions within my level designs.

Most of my IT knowledge and experience comes from my own research, learning projects and hands on exploration with computers and technology. Creating a custom boot screen on my computer took a lot of research into dual boot systems, the Linux and Windows operating systems and the Grub2 bootloader. I have also dabbled in learning program languages such as Basic, C++ and Java.

I have been interested to further my education for a while now. working a fulltime job, I have felt trapped, limited with options to further my education and career. Entering into my 30’s has provided a good push to pursue my long-term goals. I chose to study at RMIT as they provide the course I intended to study in an online format, which will allow me to keep my work commitments while I study.

I hope studying this course will prepare me for entering study into bachelor of IT. I hope to learn more about different IT industries and IT applications. Learn about tools and programs used in the industry and develop my collaboration skills. I would be particularly interested in learning more about the development of mobile technologies within the home and the workplace.
